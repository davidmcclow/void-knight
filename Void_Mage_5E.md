# The Void Mage
## D&D 5th Edition Class

*A cloaked figure stands before an army of enemy wizards. As spells rain down, the figure raises a hand—and the magic simply... stops. Where arcane power should be, there is only void.*

Void Mages are martial anti-casters who channel the absence of magic itself. They counterspell with a thought, suppress magical effects with their presence, and turn enemy magic against their foes. Part warrior, part anti-wizard, they are the bane of spellcasters everywhere.

---

## Class Features

**Hit Points**
- **Hit Dice:** 1d10 per Void Mage level
- **Hit Points at 1st Level:** 10 + your Constitution modifier
- **Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 1d10 (or 6) + your Constitution modifier per Void Mage level after 1st

**Proficiencies**
- **Armor:** Light armor, medium armor, shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Tools:** None
- **Saving Throws:** Constitution, Intelligence
- **Skills:** Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, Arcana, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Stealth

**Equipment**
You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:
- (a) chain mail or (b) leather armor, longbow, and 20 arrows
- (a) a martial weapon and a shield or (b) two martial weapons
- (a) a light crossbow and 20 bolts or (b) two handaxes
- (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack

## Class Features

### Void Pool (1st Level)
You possess a reservoir of void energy that you use to fuel your abilities. You have a number of void points equal to 4 + twice your Void Mage level (shown in the Void Mage table). When you spend a void point, it is unavailable until you finish a short or long rest, at which point all spent void points return.

Some of your void features require your target to make a saving throw. The saving throw DC is calculated as follows:

**Void Save DC** = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

**Void Attack Modifier** = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

---

### Null Zone (1st Level)
As a bonus action, you can spend 1 void point to create a 10-foot-radius aura of magical suppression centered on yourself for 1 minute. The aura moves with you.

Within this zone:
- You and allies gain a bonus to saving throws against spells and magical effects equal to the bonus shown in your Null Zone Bonus column
- You have advantage on ability checks made to counterspell
- Creatures with spell resistance or advantage on saves against magic (including you and allies) add your Null Zone Bonus to their AC against spell attacks

At 10th level, the radius increases to 15 feet.  
At 15th level, the radius increases to 20 feet.  
At 20th level, the radius increases to 30 feet.

---

### Void Strike (1st Level)
As a bonus action, you can spend 1 void point to infuse your weapon with void energy for 1 minute. While active, your weapon attacks:
- Count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity
- Deal an additional 1d6 force damage
- Can target ethereal or incorporeal creatures normally

At 5th level, once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack while Void Strike is active, you can attempt to dispel one magical effect on the target. Make an Intelligence check (DC 10 + the spell's level). On a success, the effect ends.

---

### Reactive Counterspell (2nd Level)
You can use your reaction to attempt to counter a spell being cast within 60 feet of you. To do so, you must spend void points equal to the spell's level (minimum 1 void point) and make an Intelligence check. The DC equals 10 + the spell's level. On a success, the spell fails and has no effect.

If you're uncertain what spell is being cast, you can use your reaction to identify it (Intelligence [Arcana] check, DC 10 + spell level), then decide whether to attempt the counterspell.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest. Starting at 9th level, you can spend 1 void point to use this feature even if you have no uses remaining.

---

### Fighting Style (2nd Level)
You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options:

**Dueling:** When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

**Defense:** While you are wearing armor, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

**Great Weapon Fighting:** When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property.

**Two-Weapon Fighting:** When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

---

### Void Step (3rd Level)
As a bonus action, you can spend 1 void point to teleport to an unoccupied space you can see within 30 feet. This movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks.

At 9th level, you can use this as a reaction after taking damage.  
At 15th level, the range increases to 60 feet.

---

### Spellbreaker (3rd Level)
You have learned to convert disrupted magic into fuel for your own abilities. When you successfully use Reactive Counterspell or dispel a magical effect (such as with Void Strike), you regain 1 void point. 

You can regain void points this way a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1) per long rest.

This feature represents your growing mastery as an anti-mage specialist—the more magic you shut down, the stronger you become.

---

### Void Mage Archetype (3rd Level)
Choose an archetype that shapes your approach to void magic: Void Reaver (aggressive striker), Null Guardian (defensive protector), or Null Mender (healer and support). Your choice grants you features at 3rd level and again at 7th, 14th, and 18th level.

---

### Ability Score Improvement (4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, 19th Level)
You can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores by 1. You can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Alternatively, you can take a feat.

---

### Extra Attack (5th Level)
You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

---

### Improved Void Strike (5th Level)
The force damage from your Void Strike increases to 2d6. Additionally, when you successfully dispel a magical effect using Void Strike, you regain 1 void point (maximum once per turn).

---

### Void Armor (6th Level)
When you take damage, you can use your reaction to spend 1 void point and gain temporary hit points equal to 1d10 + your Intelligence modifier. These temporary hit points last for 1 minute.

At 12th level, when you use this feature, you also gain resistance to the damage type that triggered it until the start of your next turn.

---

### Enhanced Null Zone (9th Level)
Your Null Zone grants additional benefits:
- Hostile creatures that start their turn in your Null Zone have disadvantage on Constitution saving throws to maintain concentration
- When a spell is cast within your Null Zone, the caster must make a Constitution saving throw (DC = your Void Save DC) or the spell costs an additional spell slot level (if they have no higher slot, the spell fails)
- The radius of your Null Zone increases to 15 feet (if not already larger)

---

### Spell Resistance (10th Level)
You have advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

Additionally, when you succeed on a saving throw against a spell that targets only you, you can use your reaction to reflect the spell back at the caster. The spell targets the caster using the same spell save DC, attack bonus, and spellcasting ability.

You can use this reflection ability once, and you regain the ability when you finish a short or long rest.

---

### Greater Void Strike (11th Level)
The force damage from Void Strike increases to 3d6. Additionally, when you hit a creature with a weapon attack while Void Strike is active, you can spend 2 void points to cast *greater dispel magic* on the target (using your Void Save DC).

---

### Void Mastery (13th Level)
As an action, you can spend 3 void points to create a 15-foot-radius sphere of antimagic centered on yourself for 1 minute. The sphere moves with you.

This functions like the *antimagic field* spell, but doesn't suppress your own void magic abilities. You can suppress or resume this effect as a bonus action.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

---

### Devastating Strike (15th Level)
When you hit with a weapon attack while Void Strike is active, you can spend 2 additional void points to deal an extra 4d6 force damage and force the target to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the target is stunned until the end of your next turn as void energy overwhelms their senses.

---

### Void Assault (17th Level)
As an action, you can spend 3 void points to make a devastating void-infused attack. Make a single weapon attack. On a hit, the target takes normal weapon damage plus 10d6 force damage and must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Void Save DC) or have all spell effects on them suppressed for 1 minute (as if affected by *antimagic field*). The target can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on a success.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

---

### Eternal Void (20th Level)
Your mastery of void magic is complete:
- Your Null Zone is always active and costs no void points to maintain
- You regain 1 void point at the start of each of your turns if you have fewer than half your maximum
- Your Void Step has unlimited uses and costs no void points (though you still can't use it more than once per turn unless you use it as a reaction)
- Once per turn when you hit with a weapon attack, you can end one spell of 5th level or lower on the target (no check required)

# Void Mage Archetypes

## Void Reaver
*"I don't just counter magic—I consume it."*

The Void Reaver is an aggressive anti-mage striker who turns dispelled magic into offensive power. They feed on arcane energy, growing stronger with each spell they destroy.

### Hungering Void (3rd Level)
Your Void Strike deals additional force damage. When you activate Void Strike, it deals an extra 1d6 force damage (total 2d6 at 3rd level, 3d6 at 5th, 4d6 at 13th).

Additionally, when you hit a creature with a Void Strike attack, you can spend 1 void point to attempt to dispel one magical effect on the target as part of the attack (Intelligence check, DC 10 + spell level).

---

### Predatory Step (3rd Level)
When you successfully use Reactive Counterspell or dispel a magical effect, you can immediately use Void Step as part of the same reaction without spending a void point. This Void Step must move you closer to the source of the magic.

---

### Void Frenzy (7th Level)
When you successfully counterspell or dispel a magical effect, you enter a state of enhanced focus. You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls and damage rolls for 1 minute. Each time you successfully counterspell or dispel while this bonus is active, the bonus increases by +2 (maximum +6).

This bonus ends if you go 1 minute without counterspelling or dispelling a magical effect.

---

### Devastating Void Strike (14th Level)
When you activate Void Strike, you can spend an additional 2 void points to gain the following benefits for the duration:
- Your weapon attacks ignore resistance to force damage and treat immunity as resistance
- When you hit with a weapon attack, you can choose to deal all your damage as force damage
- Your critical hit range increases by 1 (19-20, or 18-20 if you already have an improved range)

---

### Cascade of Negation (18th Level)
When you successfully counterspell or dispel a spell of 4th level or higher, you can unleash a wave of nullification. All creatures of your choice within 30 feet must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = your Void Save DC) or have one magical effect of your choice on them suppressed for 1 minute.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

---

## Null Guardian
*"Magic shall not pass."*

The Null Guardian dedicates themselves to protecting others from magical threats, serving as a living shield against hostile spellcasting.

### Expanded Null Zone (3rd Level)
Your Null Zone's benefits extend to your allies. Allies within your Null Zone gain all the benefits you do, including the bonus to saving throws against magic and advantage on counterspell checks.

Additionally, when you activate your Null Zone, choose one damage type (acid, cold, fire, lightning, or thunder). You and allies within the zone gain resistance to that damage type.

---

### Guardian's Intercept (3rd Level)
When an ally within 30 feet is targeted by a spell or harmful magical effect, you can use your reaction and spend 1 void point to become the new target of that spell or effect (if the spell allows). You gain advantage on any saving throw against the redirected spell.

At 9th level, the range increases to 60 feet and you can choose to automatically succeed on the saving throw by spending an additional void point.

---

### Sheltering Void (7th Level)
As an action, you can spend 2 void points to create a 10-foot-radius dome of void energy centered on a point you can see within 30 feet. The dome provides three-quarters cover against spells and magical effects (though physical attacks pass through normally). The dome lasts for 1 minute or until dismissed as a bonus action.

At 14th level, creatures within the dome gain your Spell Resistance feature while inside.

---

### Protective Reflexes (14th Level)
When you or an ally within your Null Zone takes damage from a spell or magical effect, you can use your reaction to reduce that damage by an amount equal to 1d10 + your Void Mage level. If you spend 1 void point, this applies to all allies within your Null Zone who took damage from the same source.

You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

---

### Bulwark of the Void (18th Level)
As an action, you can spend 3 void points to grant yourself and all allies within your Null Zone immunity to spells of 3rd level or lower for 1 minute. Spells of 4th level or higher function normally but affected creatures have advantage on saving throws against them.

Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a long rest.

---

## Null Mender
*"The void doesn't destroy—it purifies."*

The Null Mender has learned to use void energy to restore and cleanse, returning injured bodies to their "null state"—healthy and whole.

### Void Restoration (3rd Level)
You can heal by channeling void energy. As an action, you can spend void points to heal a creature you touch. The creature regains hit points based on the void points spent:

- **1 void point:** 2d8 + your Intelligence modifier (touch)
- **2 void points:** 2d8 + your Intelligence modifier (30-foot range)
- **3 void points:** 2d8 + your Intelligence modifier to all creatures of your choice in a 30-foot radius centered on you

This healing increases to 3d8 at 5th level, 4d8 at 9th level, 5d8 at 13th level, and 6d8 at 17th level.

This healing works on any creature, including constructs and undead (though undead can choose to make a Wisdom saving throw to resist).

---

### Purging Touch (3rd Level)
When you use Void Restoration, you can choose one of two effects:

**Standard Healing:** Heal for the full amount

**Purging Restoration:** Heal for half the amount (rounded down) and remove one of the following conditions from the target: blinded, charmed, deafened, frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, or stunned.

At 10th level, you can also remove one level of exhaustion or one curse.

---

### Mender's Spellbreaker (3rd Level)
When you successfully use Reactive Counterspell or dispel a magical effect, you regain 2 void points instead of 1 (from the base Spellbreaker feature at 9th level). This stacks with Spellbreaker when you reach 9th level.

---

### Restorative Aura (7th Level)
While your Null Zone is active, allies within it regain hit points equal to your Intelligence modifier at the start of their turn.

At 15th level, this increases to twice your Intelligence modifier.

---

### Swift Restoration (14th Level)
You can use Void Restoration as a bonus action by spending 1 additional void point (total cost: 2 VP for touch, 3 VP for ranged, 4 VP for burst).

Additionally, when you use Void Restoration, you can reroll any 1s on the healing dice and must use the new rolls.

---

### Perfect Restoration (18th Level)
Once per long rest, you can spend 5 void points to touch a creature and completely restore them. This functions as the *greater restoration*, *heal*, and *remove curse* spells cast simultaneously. This even works on creatures at 0 hit points (but not dead creatures).

Additionally, once per week, you can spend all your remaining void points (minimum 7) to cast *true resurrection* without material components. The creature must have been dead for no more than 1 year.

# Playing a Void Mage

## Role in the Party
Void Mages fill a unique niche as martial anti-casters. They excel at:
- Shutting down enemy spellcasters through counterspelling
- Providing magic resistance to the party via Null Zone
- Dealing consistent damage through weapon attacks enhanced by void magic
- Surviving in melee with decent hit points and armor

**Void Reavers** function as strikers who thrive in prolonged fights against casters.  
**Null Guardians** are defenders who protect allies from magical harm.  
**Null Menders** are support characters who heal and cleanse while maintaining threat control.

## Ability Score Priority

**Primary:**
- **Intelligence** - Powers your void abilities, save DCs, and counterspells
- **Strength or Dexterity** - Your weapon attacks

**Secondary:**
- **Constitution** - Hit points and concentration
- **Wisdom** - Perception and Wisdom saves

**Tertiary:**
- **Charisma** - Social interactions

## Recommended Feats

**Combat Feats:**
- **Polearm Master** - Extra bonus action attack and reaction attacks
- **Sentinel** - Lock down enemies trying to escape your Null Zone
- **Great Weapon Master** - Massive damage for two-handed builds
- **War Caster** - Maintain concentration, perform somatic components with weapons

**Utility Feats:**
- **Alert** - Act first to establish your Null Zone
- **Mobile** - Enhanced battlefield mobility with Void Step
- **Resilient (Wisdom)** - Better saves against mental effects
- **Mage Slayer** - Even better at hunting casters

## Combat Tactics

### Opening Round
1. Activate Null Zone (bonus action, 1 VP)
2. Activate Void Strike if needed (bonus action, 1 VP) or Attack
3. Position to maximize Null Zone coverage

### Ongoing Combat
- Ready Reactive Counterspell for enemy casters
- Use Void Step to close on priority targets or escape danger
- Dispel magical effects with Void Strike attacks
- Use Void Armor when taking big hits

### Resource Management
- Start with Null Zone for defensive benefits
- Use Void Strike selectively (lasts 1 minute)
- Save void points for critical counterspells
- Spellbreaker helps sustain your pool in long fights

# Roleplaying Your Void Mage

## Character Concepts

**The Mage Hunter**
- Former victim of magical abuse who learned to fight back
- Hunts rogue spellcasters and magical criminals
- Believes magic users must be held accountable

**The Scholar of Absence**
- Studied the philosophical concept of "nothing" and discovered void magic
- Fascinated by antimagic and dispelling
- Views void as a natural balance to magical excess

**The Reluctant Anti-Mage**
- Wanted to be a wizard but lacked the gift
- Discovered void magic as an alternative path
- Still respects magic but knows its dangers

**The Guardian**
- Sworn to protect others from magical threats
- Null Guardian who sees their abilities as a sacred duty
- Typically lawful, values order and protection

**The Destroyer**
- Void Reaver who revels in negating magic
- May have a personal vendetta against spellcasters
- Aggressive, direct, results-oriented

- Null Mender who sees void as purification, not destruction

- Compassionate but pragmatic

# Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I multiclass Void Mage with spellcasting classes?**  
A: Yes, but there's thematic tension. Your void abilities don't interfere with spellcasting, but your character might have interesting internal conflicts about using both magic and antimagic.

**Q: Does Null Zone affect my allies' spells?**  
A: No! Your Null Zone protects against hostile magic but doesn't interfere with allied spellcasting. However, enemies in your zone will have a harder time casting.

**Q: What happens if two Void Mages have overlapping Null Zones?**  
A: The bonuses don't stack, but the zones can overlap peacefully. Use whichever bonus is higher.

**Q: Can I counterspell cantrips?**  
A: Yes, though it costs a minimum of 1 void point. This might not be efficient unless it's a crucial cantrip.

**Q: How does Void Step interact with difficult terrain?**  
A: Void Step is teleportation, so it ignores difficult terrain entirely.

**Q: Can Void Restoration heal undead and constructs in 5E?**  
A: Yes! This is unique to the Null Mender and reflects returning things to their "null state"—which for constructs and undead means functional/intact.
